<h1> This popular DC bike lane may be raized by the federal government</h1>
 
 Local bike advocates are concerned the federal government may be looking to axe portions of the bike lane on 15th Street down by the National Mall. 
 
The Washington Area Bicyclist Association's advocacy director Kalli Krumpos said an anonymous source within the Trump administration tipped WABA off about the potential demolition. Krumpos said designating pedestrian areas on roadways lessens congestion and lowers pedestrian crash frequency. Getting rid of the 15th street bike lane won’t help anyone, she said.

In D.C., the guidance comes at a time when bicyclist crash injuries are on the rise. While the total number of vehicle crashes has gone down each year since 2023, data from the Metro Police Department shows riding a bike on the streets of the nation’s capital is becoming more dangerous. 

The department regularly publishes traffic collision data on their website, including information like location, number of people injured, extent of injury and more. An analysis of that data shows an increase in crashes which injured or killed a bicyclist each year since 2022. 

Last year, crashes which injured or killed a bicyclist accounted for 3.1% of the total reported crashes in D.C. That’s up from just 1.6% of the total in 2022.
